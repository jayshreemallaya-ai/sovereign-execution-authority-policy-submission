# Sovereign Execution Authority (SEA)

A formal submission to the Department of Communications and Digital Technologies, South Africa.

## South African National AI Policy Submission

Submission proposing Sovereign Execution Authority (SEA) as a runtime governance substrate.

---

## Overview

This repository contains a formal submission addressing a critical gap in AI systems:

While decisions may be made on valid context, execution often occurs later—without verifying whether that context is still valid.

---

## The Problem

**Authority Drift**

AI systems act on permissions that may no longer be valid at the moment of execution.

---

## The Proposal

**Sovereign Execution Authority (SEA)**

A real-time, non-bypassable execution layer that determines whether an action is still admissible at the exact moment it occurs.

At the execution boundary, every action is:

- Approve  
- Deny  
- Halt  

---

## Positioning

This submission introduces Execution Governance as a distinct layer in AI systems, separate from model intelligence and policy frameworks.

It reframes governance as an enforceable, real-time control mechanism at the moment of action.
